What is a Digital Product Passport?
Think of it as a fact sheet for your clothes.

A Digital Product Passport is like a label on a product, but much more detailed. It tells you where the materials came from, how the item was made, how far it travelled, and what to do with it when you are finished.

All of this information is checked by an independent organisation, so you know it is true.

About this data
How we calculated these numbers
▼
What is counted: Everything from growing the cotton to getting the t-shirt to the store. This includes farming, spinning, dyeing, sewing, and shipping.

What is not counted: Washing, drying and ironing when you use it, and what happens when you are done with it. Over time, these can add up to more pollution than making the t-shirt did.

Where the numbers come from: Carbon data from the Higg Index. Water data from the Water Footprint Network. We compare this t-shirt to a standard cotton t-shirt.
